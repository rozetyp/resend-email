How to Send One-Off Emails with Resend (Without Code)

Published Nov 16, 2025 ¬∑ 5 min read

You've just set up a Resend account and verified your domain. Now you want to send a quick test email ‚Äî but you don't want to write Node.js code or mess with the CLI. Here are two ways to do it without touching your code editor.

Why Send Emails Without Code?

There are several reasons you might want to send a one-off email without writing code:

  • Testing: Verify your domain setup is working before integrating Resend into your app.
  • Manual campaigns: Send quick announcements or follow-ups to your users.
  • Debugging: Test email templates before you deploy them.
  • Speed: No need to spin up a development environment or run a script.

Method 1: Resend's Official Dashboard

Resend offers a built-in email composer in their dashboard. Here's how:

Step 1: Log Into Resend

Go to resend.com and sign in to your account.

Step 2: Navigate to Emails

Click on the Emails section in the sidebar. You'll see a list of sent emails and an option to compose new ones.

Step 3: Compose Your Email

Click New Email (or similar button). Fill in:

  • From: A verified domain in your account (e.g., noreply@example.com)
  • To: The recipient's email
  • Subject: Your email subject
  • Body: Plain text or HTML

Step 4: Send

Click Send. Resend will relay your email immediately.

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitation: Resend's dashboard is minimal. For richer formatting (bold, links, lists, etc.), you'll need to write HTML or use a rich text editor.

Method 2: Use Resend Pad (No Code, Rich Text Editor)

Resend Pad is a free, open-source web app that gives you a Gmail-like UI for sending Resend emails without code.

Step 1: Go to Resend Pad

Visit resendpad.up.railway.app (or self-host it ‚Äî it's on GitHub).

Step 2: Paste Your API Key

Get your Resend API key from resend.com/api-keys. Paste it into the "Your Resend API Key" field. Your key is never stored ‚Äî it's only used in your browser for this session.

Step 3: Load Your Domains

Click Load Verified Domains. Resend Pad will fetch your verified domains and pre-fill the "From" field.

Step 4: Fill in the Email

Enter:

  • To: Recipient email
  • Subject: Email subject
  • Body: Use the rich text editor to format your email (bold, links, lists, etc.)

Step 5: Send

Click Send Email. Resend Pad proxies your request to Resend and shows you the email ID.

Common Use Cases

Use Case 1: Test Email Delivery

You just verified a domain in Resend. Send yourself a test email to confirm everything is working.

Solution: Use Resend Pad or the Resend Dashboard. Takes 60 seconds.

Use Case 2: Quick Marketing Email

You want to send a newsletter or announcement to your audience without touching code.

Solution: Resend Pad (rich text editor) or write HTML in the Resend Dashboard. Send immediately.

Use Case 3: Debug Email Template

Your password reset email looks broken in production. You want to test a new template before rolling it out.

Solution: Paste your updated HTML into Resend Pad, send a test, and verify it looks right. Then update your code.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this free?

Yes! Resend Pad is completely free. (Resend charges for actual emails sent, but that's independent of Resend Pad.)

Is my API key safe?

Yes. Resend Pad's API key is never stored on a server. It lives in your browser and is only used for that specific send request.

Can I use this for production sending?

Yes, but consider it a testing / manual sending tool. For production (high volume, automation), integrate Resend into your code.

What if I don't have a domain yet?

You'll need a verified domain to send with Resend. Namecheap or Porkbun offer affordable domains. Once you buy one, set it up in Resend and you're ready. (Affiliate links ‚Äî we may earn a small commission)
